Empowering Entrepreneurship & Innovation

Who is entrepreneur? What is entrepreneurship?

Entrepreneur is an innovator who creates a new business, bearing most of the risks and enjoying most of the rewards. Entrepreneurship is a process of designing, launching and managing a new business venture in order to gain profit by taking several risks in the corporate world. Business world is an ocean and entrepreneurs are the swimmers. So a entrepreneur should have a strong base and skills to tackle the problems and succeed to survive in this ocean.

To create Awareness on empowering entrepreneurship and innovation among young minds MERIISE organized a workshop on 30th august 2018 in Alumni hall of MCE. The resource person-prof. V. B. Nanda Gopal (chief-executive, Centre for Start-up, Innovation and Incubation, STEP, JSS Academy of technical education, Bangalore.) is an expert in this field. He came up with an idea on entrepreneurship team in MCE and rechristened STEP to MERIISE (Malnad Enclave, Research, Innovation, Incubation, Start-up and entrepreneurship). He addressed the students and faculties about emphasizing the need to nurture innovative ideas of young minds and convert these ideas into a successful business to respond to the emerging needs of the society. Students understood the function of an entrepreneur in the successful, commercial applications of innovations. The workshop was fruitful as students got motivated towards the entrepreneurial career.

Ideation and Proof of Concept

"Ideation is the mode of the design process in which the participants concentrate on idea generation. It represents a process of 'going wide' interms of concepts and outcomes. Ideation provides both the fuel and also the source material for building prototype and getting innovative solutions into the hands of users".

ME-RIISE had organized a one-day state level event called 'IDEATION' on 16th of October 2019, where the participants has to process and generate their ideas or solutions to the problem statements which includes brief description of the concepts, feasibility of the idea and the road map of the plan till the execution. It creates volume and variety in participant's innovation option and also encourages students to convert their ideas into reality.

The event was held at ME-RIISE Office and was coordinated by Dr. A Geetha Kiran ,HOD of Dept. CSE and Mr. Vasanth Kumar ,Asst. Proff. MCA. The event evoked by watering the plants. The juries were Mr Sanjeev P Varute ,Consult, startups on IOT Bengaluru and Mr Ramesh Adiga ,Vice president and Delivery head Skava at Infosys Company.

The audience consisted of students from all the branches to dig and explore their heads in entrepreneurship. It was the first phase of the competition . Totally 100 students were participated and they were divided into 40 teams. Students conferred their ideas on various topics which included the present day problems.

The appraisal was based on the quality, reliability, ingenuity of ideas, feasibility of problems and the presentation standards. The juries received around 40 novel ideas from the students. Awards and prizes for the winners of the first phase ,IDEATION we're announced and 7 teams shortlisted by the juries for the final phase (i.e., Proof of concept). The students of Jorhat College ,Assam emerged victorious in this phase.

The session included presentation, brainstorming session and finally defining the parameters to identify an idea with enough business potential that can be taken forward as a startup. At the end of the session, participants were very happy and gave their feedback individually there by played their roles in marking this event as a grand success.

PROOF OF CONCEPT

"A proof of concept is an exercise, in which work is focused on determining whether an idea can be turned into a reality. A proof of concept is meant to determine the feasibility of an idea or to verify that the new idea will function as envisioned".

The first phase of the event IDEATION was successfully piloted by ME-RIISE. The idea faced a straighten line of questioning in the screening stage and the best ideas were shortlisted for the next phase that is 'PROOF OF CONCEPT'. The proof of concept sought to test a basic architecture proposal for the system that would support this goal. The shortlisted teams were given around three months time to come out with a product /prototype /business model /solutions. Proof of concept helps to give weight to an idea and guages the concept in scaling it as market ready. Formulating Proof of concept is a giant step towards cognizance of an idea. It allows for detailed probe and provides a true litmus test for feasibility. Totally five teams had participated in this event. Cloud Literacy day

Cloud Literacy Day is intended to induce self-learning and introduce basic cloud technology skills to all students. It is imperative for students to learn the cloud technology skills to make them on par with industry. where the students learn the modules online through self-instructed videos and exercises and prove their cloud learning skills

ME RIISE organised a one day workshop on 31st October 2019 at college auditorium for students from various departments of MCE. The workshop was conducted by ICT Academy in association with AWS Educate.

The main moto of ICT Academy is to Create New Generation Talent Pool. ICT Academy is an initiative of the Government of India in collaboration with the state Governments and Industries. ICT Academy is a not-for-profit society, the first of its kind pioneer venture under the Public-Private-Partnership (PPP) model that endeavors to train the higher education teachers and students thereby exercises on developing the next generation teachers and industry ready students.

The event Inauguration started by watering the plants and introducing the speaker to all the students was done by Dr. A Geetha Kiran. The speaker from ICT Academy started his talk with an marvelous story of how their ICT Academy grew and what is the moto of it and what they are going to teach on that entire day. He also briefed about their program that this introductory self learning course explores how computers work and Internet infrastructure, cloud computing models and AWS fundamentals. Throughout the course, students will expand their knowledge of cloud computing through narrative-based scenarios and short interactive tasks. Students will also survey cloud careers and discuss industry demand for cloud skills. Students learnt some basic concepts on cloud using the modules online through self-instructed videos and exercises and prove their cloud learning skills

At the end of the day all the students learnt to evaluate and assess the benefits of cloud computing and cloud applications. Those students who successfully completed the course, they earned their first AWS Inventor Badge & AWS 101 Introduction to Cloud Computing.

It was such a magnificent and immensely helpful session, where the participants were able to gain knowledge about basic cloud technology skills and also were enthusiastically participated to gain their AWS Inventor badge.

Big Data Workshop

What is big data? Why is big data?

Big Data is a field that treats ways to analyse, systematically extract information from, or otherwise deal with data sets that are too large or complex to be dealt with by traditional data-processing application software. Data with many cases(rows) offer greater statistical power, while data with higher complexity(more attributes or columns)may lead to higher false discovery rate.

Without big data analytics, most of the organisations would become similar to a wayfarer, who doesn't know what to do or where to go completely lost in the world of data. Big data is like a compass to the wanderer.

With knowing all this advantages of big data, “PRAGYATHA” provided a great opportunity for interested candidates from all over the state who were eager to learn the basics of “Big Data” analytics.

Numerous participants from all over the state were gathered to harness the knowledge from the workshop which lasted for two consecutive days during “PRAGYATHA”.

The participants were monitored by a very resourceful person named Dr. Pramod Kumar Naik, who has been working as a Senior manager for Technical and skill development division at "AGIMUS Technologies Pvt. Ltd" and “RV Skills”, Bangalore, guided the participants in developing the technical skills on Big Data.

It was indeed a promising workshop with positive feedback from all the participants.
